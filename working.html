Welcome to our wedding website and for the technologically challenged guests, congratulations for making it this far!

We are hoping that this website will be able to provide everyone with all of the necessary information, as well as allow them to RSVP.

Meals, registry and even favours are going to be done a little differently than the norm, but we hope to provide an explanation for why we have chosen to do things in this way. The most important thing to Daniel and I is that we are able to spend the day with the people we love, the people who have supported us through our relationship and who have helped us grow into the quasi adults we have become today… and also that we end up married at the end of the day.

Above, you will be able to find information about the venue (including hotels and campsites nearby), the program, what we have chosen to do with the favours and registry, as well as a section to RSVP and make a wine selection! Happy browsing, and try not to become too overwhelmed by the incandescence of our eternal love displayed via our incredibly adorable engagement photos… thank you Sarah Robinson!

Favours

Instead of giving out favours we have decided to donate the money we would have spent to Raise and Support the Poor.

RSP is a non-governmental organization in Cambodia. In 2012 I had the opportunity to travel to Cambodia and work with Sareth Brak, the Buddhist monk who runs the program. We spent two weeks in the village where Sareth lives and helped construct the second floor of the library for the local school. I learned many new skills in those two weeks, like how to bend wire for concrete posts while avoiding being eaten alive by the red ants who live in the bending block, or how to catch bricks as they are being tossed from the ground; after first being checked for lizards and snakes. However, I would say the most valuable thing I took from the experience was seeing a functional NGO that produced significant results and really made the most of the funding they received. Sareth’s commitment to the community was pretty darn inspiring, and I fully trust him to allocate the money to whatever area of the organization he thinks needs it most. So I can’t promise you that the money will buy new bikes for the kids in the village, or will pay for the concrete used in the construction of the new kitchen. It might go toward paying very boring and unromantic administrative costs. But wherever it ends up, it will be money well spent, and I thank you in advance for the favour you gave up to make the donation possible!

Registry

After much deliberation, which mostly consisted of me telling Daniel this was a non-negotiable, we had decided not to register with any stores online. Because Daniel and I have both lived away from home off and on for quite some time, we are pretty well set up home wise. Daniel’s kitchen dowry basically takes care of any baking needs we will ever have. I also have a somewhat cynical view that the wedding industry’s main purpose is to convince us to buy things we never needed, or even wanted, for 500 million dollars more than these things are worth. Realistically, because we will both be going back to school in the fall, what we need more than anything else is money to pay for tuition. I know the idea of giving money is not very exciting or romantic, but Daniel and I are both boring and practical people, who love the idea of having needs met before wants (perhaps and I am just a little bit more boring and practical). So if you fall in the group of people who are comfortable giving money, that is much appreciated. However, if you find that you have just turned purple in the face by the utter and complete rudeness of what we just asked, and are now contemplating whether you will even be coming to the wedding at all, first, please, take three deep breaths. Second, know that we would rather have you at the wedding and happy than not at all, so don’t worry about money or gifts and just bring your wonderful self! And remember, keep breathing! If however, you find yourself in a third group, where you are not exactly purple and horrified, but feel disappointed in not being able to give gifts (which I understand is a very important love language), than we will be registering at the Bay or Sears for a few small items, and we will be creating our own registry at MEC. Unfortunately we will have to provide links to the products as MEC does not have a registry at this time. Awesome finds of Kijiji are also much appreciated.

Final Note

I know the writing on the website has been a little dry and sarcastic throughout, this is my first time writing something for more than just my T.A. or professor, and I can get a little cynical when I’m nervous. But in all seriousness, we are so excited to spend and share this day with all of you. I love a good story with an interesting plot and dynamic characters. And while Daniel and I might be the main protagonists of this particular narrative, each and every one of you has played a part in filling in the gaps, in making it an unpredictable and exciting adventure, that above all, is a story about love. I think Daniel would agree that we would not be capable of loving each other half as well, if we weren’t first supported and loved by so many of you. Thank you for the part you played in making our story a great one.
